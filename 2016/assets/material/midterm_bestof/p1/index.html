The journey to a Malaria free africa

Malaria is both preventable and curable, and increased efforts are dramatically reducing the malaria burden in many places. Malaria is caused by the Plasmodium parasite and is transmitted by female Anopheles mosquitoes which bite between dusk and dawn. People infected with malaria often experience fever, chills and flu-like illness at first. Left untreated, the disease can lead to severe complications and, in some cases, death.
